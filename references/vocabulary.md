# Vocabulary

Words and phrases I use, and ones I don't. Pulled from my LinkedIn posts.

## Words and phrases I use
- **tl;dr** — lowercase, with the colon. Used to flag the one-sentence version of a long post.
- **dropped** — as in "I just dropped a 1-hour masterclass". Casual, present-tense launch verb.
- **masterclass** — for free long-form video content.
- **stack / scale** — "scaled that business to 7-figures". Plain past-tense.
- **runway** — financial cushion in months. Always in the founder sense.
- **cost per lead**, **close rate**, **conversion rate**, **split test** — named flatly. No "CPL" or other acronyms unless I've spelled them once.
- **on-page SEO**, **technical SEO**, **topical authority**, **domain authority**, **service pages**, **money keywords** — industry-standard terms used without quotation marks.
- **AI slop** — generic AI-generated content. Always pejorative.
- **rising tide / boats that rise with the tide** — preferred metaphor for topical authority lifting service pages.
- **same game, different wrapper** — for "this isn't new, just rebranded".
- **bottle the flow** — package a repeatable process into a skill/command.
- **the mechanic** — the actual how, as opposed to the dream.
- **happy path / error path** — when describing testing.
- **lever** — used sparingly, as in "the biggest lever". Never "leverage" as a verb.
- **operator** — preferred over "entrepreneur" or "founder-influencer".

## Numbers and units
- Dollar amounts as `$1.2M`, `$500K`, `$10K`, `$2K`. Capital `K` and `M`, no space.
- Multiples as `4x`, `7x`, `10x`. Lowercase x, no space.
- Percentages as `20%`, `33%`. No space.
- Time as `1.25 hour`, `45-second`, `10-second`, `1-hour`. Hyphenate when used as a modifier.
- Use arrows for transformation: `$200 → $30`, `$10K → $6K`. Unicode →, with spaces.
- Counts get the number, not the word: `9 video testimonials`, `2 weeks`, `8 cities`, `6 years`.

## Formatting tells
- **Unicode-bold** (𝐈, 𝐦𝐚𝐬𝐭𝐞𝐫𝐜𝐥𝐚𝐬𝐬) is used for LinkedIn post hooks. Not for body text. Not for blog/website.
- `👉` once at the top of a LinkedIn post, paired with the one-sentence value prop. Never used in body or in non-social writing.
- `→` for sequences and transformations. Used freely.
- Numbered lists for "here's what's inside" sections.
- Em dashes are fine. No double-hyphens.

## Words I avoid
- **Game-changer, unlock, leverage (verb), synergy, needle-mover, ninja, rockstar, guru, hack (as noun, e.g. "growth hack"), 10x your X.**
- **Crushing it, killing it, absolutely.**
- **Just** as a softener ("I just wanted to…"). Cut it.
- **Very, really, super** as intensifiers. Cut or replace with a number.
- **In today's fast-paced world**, **at the end of the day**, **let that sink in**, **food for thought**.
- **Solutions** (vague). Name the thing.
- **Reach out** — say "email me" or "DM me".
- **Synergize, ideate, circle back, touch base, drill down.**
- **Utilize** — use "use".
- **Leverage** as a verb — use "use" or name the mechanic.
- **Plethora, myriad, robust, holistic, seamless, cutting-edge, world-class.**

## Capitalisation and spelling
- Australian/British spelling where it sits naturally (optimisation/optimization both okay depending on audience; default to the one used in the surrounding piece).
- Brand names exactly as the brand writes them: **Claude Code**, **Semrush**, **Google Lighthouse**, **Supabase**, **Vercel**, **Next.js**, **Tailwind CSS**, **ChatGPT**, **Perplexity**.
- Sentence case for headings, not title case.
