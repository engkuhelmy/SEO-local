# Tone

How my writing should feel. Built from my LinkedIn posts.

## Overall feel
- Direct, plainspoken, confident without being cocky.
- Operator energy, not guru energy. I've done the thing; I'm telling you how.
- Honest about failure. Specific about numbers. Light on adjectives.
- Reads like a founder talking to another founder over coffee, not a marketer pitching.

## Voice rules
- **Short sentences.** One idea per line. Break paragraphs early.
- **Use real numbers.** $1.2M, 50,000 clicks/month, 20% conversion, $200 → $30, 4x close rate. Numbers carry the credibility — never hand-wave.
- **Concrete over abstract.** "9 video testimonials" beats "social proof". "45-second founder video" beats "personal branding".
- **Show the runway, not the result.** Tension first ($10K → $6K → $4K → $2K), payoff second.
- **First person, present-tense reflection** when telling a story. "I'm down to my last 2K." Pulls the reader into the moment.
- **Plain English.** Explain like the reader is 15 and has no coding background. No jargon without an immediate translation.
- **One clear action per piece.** What do I want the reader to do next.

## Structure I lean on
- Bold one-liner hook at the top (often Unicode-bold for LinkedIn).
- A 👉 or → line that compresses the offer into one sentence.
- A scene-set: time, place, sensory detail. ("Sunday afternoon, super sunny outside.")
- A descent: stakes get worse in tight one-line beats.
- A turn: one line where something breaks.
- A list of specifics — numbered, each with a real metric.
- A close that's flat, almost throwaway. No motivational summary.

## What I avoid
- "Game-changer", "unlock", "leverage" (as a verb), "synergy", "10x your…", "let that sink in", "needle-mover".
- LinkedIn-influencer cadence: one-sentence paragraphs stacked into a faux-poem with no substance.
- Emojis as decoration. A single 👉 or → for function only.
- Hedging ("I think maybe possibly"). Either I know it or I say I don't.
- Selling the dream. I sell the mechanic.
- Long preambles. Get to the thing.

## Rhythm
- Mix one-line punches with the occasional longer sentence that earns its length by carrying a real point.
- White space is a tool. Use it.
- End sections on a flat, declarative line. No bow on top.

## Tells that it's me
- "The tl;dr is this:"
- "Here's a few details inside:"
- "Old way: … New way: …" framing.
- Naming a specific tool by name (Claude Code, Semrush, Google Lighthouse, Supabase, Vercel) rather than saying "an SEO tool".
- "100% free" stated plainly at the end of a value drop.
