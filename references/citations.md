# Citation directories · the safe starting list

> Citations = your business listed (NAP: Name · Address · Phone) on third-party directories. Google cross-references these to verify your business is real. Consistent NAP across 30-50 directories beats messy NAP across 300.

---

## The golden rule

**NAP must be byte-identical everywhere.** Pick one format and never deviate.

- Name: exact spelling — no extra "LLC" on some, "Inc" on others
- Address: pick "St" OR "Street" — never mix
- Phone: pick `(416) 555-0142` OR `416-555-0142` — never mix
- Website: pick `https://www.example.com` OR `https://example.com` — never mix

One inconsistency = Google treats it as a different business. 50 inconsistencies = your local pack ranking tanks.

```
   ↓
   ├─→ Major anchors: Google · Apple Maps · Bing · Yelp · Facebook · Foursquare · TripAdvisor · YellowPages
   └─→ Data aggregators: Data Axle · Foursquare API · Localeze · Acxiom · Here · TomTom
                ↓
                └─→ ~70 downstream sites pulling from those aggregators (Snapchat, Uber, Waze, in-car GPS, mobile apps, regional directories)
```

**Net effect:** one form → ~95 listings · NAP byte-identical across all of them · auto-sync on changes.

## Submission strategy

### Manual (free · time-intensive)

Submit each directory by hand. ~5-10 minutes per listing × 30 listings = **3-5 hours total**. Free.

### Semi-automated (paid services)

| Service | Cost | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **SEMRush Listing Management** | $20-40/mo | One form → submits to 70+ directories · auto-detects inconsistencies |
| **BrightLocal Citation Builder** | $3-9 per citation | One-time submissions · manual review |
| **Whitespark Local Citation Finder** | $20/mo | Finds gaps + submits |
| **Yext** | $200-500/yr | Real-time syncing across 70+ directories |
| **Moz Local** | $99-179/yr | Distribution + monitoring |

**Recommendation:** SEMRush Listing Management is the best value. Whitespark for citation gap analysis. Yext only if you have 10+ locations.

### Hybrid (recommended for solos / small businesses)

1. Manually submit Tier 1 + Tier 2 (the 25 most important · free)
2. Run a SEMRush Listing Management scan to find gaps + submit to long tail
3. Manually submit Tier 4 (industry-specific) — these matter more than long-tail
4. Set a quarterly reminder to re-check NAP consistency

---

## What NOT to do

- ❌ **Don't submit to 500+ random directories.** Spam citations dilute the signal · some get penalized.
- ❌ **Don't use different NAP variations.** "Acme Co" on one, "Acme Company Inc" on another = confused Google.
- ❌ **Don't pay for "1000 citations for $50" services.** They submit to garbage sites · can trigger manual review.
- ❌ **Don't skip the data aggregators.** Tier 3 saves 50+ hours of manual work.
- ❌ **Don't ignore industry directories.** Industry-specific authority > 50 generic citations.

---

## Quarterly maintenance

Every 3 months, run this checklist:

- [ ] WebSearch your business name + city · check first 2 pages
- [ ] Check NAP consistency on top 10 listings (any drift?)
- [ ] Update hours if seasonal
- [ ] Add any new locations to ALL directories
- [ ] Remove duplicate listings (Google penalizes duplicates)
- [ ] Re-run SEMRush Listing Scan if subscribed
